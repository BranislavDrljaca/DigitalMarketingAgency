Terms in digital marketing


DIGITAL MARKETING – part of marketing that deals with creating an online / digital strategy in order to brand and improve the company's presence and visibility through as many digital channels as possible. Activities relate to:

- creating web content,

- search engine optimization (SEO),

- presence and activity on social networks,

- e-mail marketing,

- advertising on social networks and search engines (SEM).

DIGITAL MARKETING CHANNELS – SEO, paid advertising, content marketing, email marketing, mobile marketing, social networks, digital display, banners, analytics.

SEO – Search Engine Optimization – website optimization for search engines and search engine positioning as the most relevant result. Due to the enormous production of content on the Internet, search engines had to enable the user to get the "highest quality" result for their search term. By optimizing your website based on rules and factors, and certain techniques and methods, ensure yourself a better position on the search engine. Optimization gives you organic and lasting results.

SEM – Search engine marketing – search engine marketing refers to the positioning of a website through:

organic positioning – SEO
paid positioning – PPC.

PAID ADVERTISING – advertising on search engines, portals, social networks.

GOOGLE ADS – Google's paid advertising system, which allows you to create ads to attract users when they search for specific products or services.

GOOGLE ANALYTICS – a free google tool to track traffic statistics for your website.

SOCIAL NETWORKS – Platforms for user interconnection. The most famous are Facebook, Instagram, Youtube, Twitter, Snapchat, LinkedIn, Pinterest, Tumblr…

SEARCH ENGINE – search engine / specialized search engine whose main purpose is to search for information found on other internet sites. The most famous are: Google, Bing. Yahoo! Searcg, Ask.com, Baidu, Yandex…

CONTENT – the content of web pages (articles, blogs, infographics, photos, videos, etc.) which is further published on other digital marketing channels.

CONTENT MARKETING – combines marketing strategies with content publishing and distribution activities. It aims to create content that will be relevant, interesting to the user, and interest the user to buy / consume a service or product. Content needs to be created following the rules and trends of search engines for better positioning on the same.

CONTENT STRATEGY – Content planning, development and management process.

E-MAIL MARKETING – the use of e-mail as a means of communication between the company and users. It is a form of direct marketing because it serves to promote services or products. It is also a system in which the user subscribes to the list, the so-called newsletter, which directly provides information about the company, products, services, and is a way of caring for customers and promotes loyalty.

NEWSLETTER – notification sent by e-mail, to the list of subscribers who have previously voluntarily left their data and subscribed to the list.

TARGET AUDIENCE – target audience, creating profiles of users for whom a particular product or service is intended.

URL – in the generally accepted sense it represents a web address..

PPC – Pay per click – pay per click. A campaign model, where you as an advertiser pay a certain amount to search engines every time someone clicks on your ad. The great advantages of this model are: immediate results, pay per click, the ability to stop campaigns without losses, if they do not project the expected return on investment.
